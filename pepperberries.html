Pepperberries

Tasmanian Pepperberries (Tasmannia lanceolata) and otherwise referred to as Mountain or Bush Pepper. They are packed full of antioxidants and other herbal properties including anti inflammatory and food preservation.




Endemic to Tasmania, Victoria & New South Wales highlands, these are natively grown in the Huon Valley, harvested by hand and naturally dried.

We ensure we share some with the local Currawongs who enjoy a feed on these beautiful native berries! Often a sign they are ripe to pick…

Our dried Tasmanian Pepperberries come in 35g glass jars, we believe this is a perfect size for those new to using them. We have deliberately not placed a sticky label on the jars so that they can be easily reused, recycled or returned to us to reuse. The easiest way to use is to think - pepper addition or substitute. Place into a pepper grinder (you may need to crush a little bit first depending on your grinder) and use with or instead of regular black pepper. We suggest using small amounts first and then add more to your taste. We like to crack it over peanut butter on toast, in ginger and lemongrass tea, on vanilla ice cream, in Gin & Tonic (it adds a nice purple colour), in preservatives (salsa, pasta sauce, relish etc…), on meat/tofu/vegetables, in stew, soups, mash and anywhere else you would use regular black pepper.
